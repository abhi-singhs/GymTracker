## Design Context

### Users
This product is for a single primary user: the owner of the app. They use it as a personal gym companion to log workouts, monitor progress, set goals, and stay consistent over time. The interface should support quick check-ins before, during, and after workouts, while also making longer planning and review sessions feel clear and rewarding.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel motivating, informative, and beautiful. Its emotional tone should be calm and focused rather than loud or gamified. It should help the user feel in control, capable, and steadily improving.

### Aesthetic Direction
Design the app as a refined performance journal: warm, athletic, and structured without feeling harsh. Support both light and dark themes from the start. Favor a premium editorial feel with confident typography, tactile controls, and progress surfaces that feel deliberate rather than dashboard-generic. Avoid noisy neon-fitness aesthetics, gimmicky gamification, and overly techy or sterile visuals.

### Design Principles
1. Build for momentum: every screen should make it easy to continue the next healthy action.
2. Keep the interface calm under effort: visual hierarchy should stay clear even when the user is tired or moving quickly.
3. Make progress tangible: training streaks, volume, consistency, and goals should feel rewarding and legible.
4. Prefer structured warmth over generic fitness tropes: polished, athletic, and personal beats flashy or aggressive.
5. Design offline-first interactions that feel immediate and trustworthy, with sync treated as supportive infrastructure.
